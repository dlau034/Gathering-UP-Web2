About GatheringUP

GatheringUP is an exciting website that is dedicated to sharing your experiences & finding interesting things to do in and around Central Park. Users can read and discover other people's experiences through stories, as well as contribute their own stories using our unique narrative building platform.

GatheringUP takes existing geographical neighborhoods to create a digital environment where people can explore new locations, discover up & coming events and socially engage each other through sharing content. GatheringUP aims captures the personality & uniqueness of our experiences in parks & neighborhoods.

Meet Our Team

Kenneth Su

FOUNDER

While most children sought to become Astronauts or Presidents of the United States, Ken, wanted to be a mathematician as a child. As he grew older, he realized that he had a gift for math, but wasn't really that passionate about it. That dream was finally laid to rest when he took a Linear Algebra course in college and only managed to get a 'D'. He realized then, that he wasn't that talented and decided to learn more about computers instead.

Since graduating college, Ken has taken a scorched earth tactic to his career and has had a total of nine full time jobs and two consulting jobs. Seven of those jobs were in large global financial companies. The most exciting and stressful was working at Barclay's capital during the financial crisis as Barclay's purchased Lehman Brothers. The most memorable was a one year stint working for HP in Singapore.

After suffering through long hours and stressful working conditions in Wall St, he finally decided to take the plunge to create his own startup. He figured that starting a company can't be as stressful as working on a trading floor... or can it?

David Lau

LEAD DESIGNER

David is the Lead Designer at GatheringUP. Originally from Auckland, New Zealand, David has always had a passion for visual design & technology. He takes pride designing using a mixture of traditional visual design techniques & web rapid prototyping to produce great user experience & interfaces.

In 2012, David took the opportunity to move to the states, to work and learn from the vibrant tech community in New York City. Since coming over, he has worked with other New York based companies such as RedRover and Techkinesis, before landing his current position at GatheringUP. He is a self-taught coder, striving to learn more about front-end web development trends & technology and enjoys competing in hackathons.

Ashley Villarreal

Writer

Born, but not raised in Texas, Ashley spent the first thirteen years of her life living in cities across the Southern U.S., Mexico and Spain. The most recent thirteen years have been split (somewhat unfairly) between Arizona and New York City.

Ashley developed a deep obsession with storytelling from a young age, which later propelled her to seek a B.A. in Journalism from the University of Arizona in 2008. She worked for a few glorious years as an underpaid journalist covering everything from politics, health care and the arts before seeking full-time work in project management at a large international nonprofit.

After several years spent working in research and program development, she's returned to her journalistic roots to dedicate her time and skills to building strategic communications for creative ventures.

In her free time, she cooks, eats and just thinks about food. She also never misses an opportunity to thrift interesting things in NYC and repurpose used goods for better use.

Matthew Caprioli

Writer

Matt(hew) was born in California, grew up in Alaska, went to college in Massachusetts, and now lives in New York. For most his life, Matthew thought he would be a professional figure skater. Realizing at 13 that this was social suicide, Matthew turned to academics, mainly English.

He went to Clark University, met people hailing from all over the world, and did a lot of mattress surfing. He eventually moved back to Anchorage, Alaska and worked a research assistant for a social research company, where he learned fun facts like it takes 2 to 3 days for people in Bethel, Alaska to send one email.

He joined GatheringUP because he wants to hear personal stories or, “news of the human condition.” Matthew is also a music writer for Quip Magazine. His personal projects include learning to play the piano, trying to speak Spanish and French, and reading everything in the world. Matthew is happy to speak with literally anyone, so shoot him an email sometime.

Jeremy Klein

Writer

Aside from the four years spent getting his History degree from the University of Vermont, Jeremy has always lived in suburban Nanuet, New York (it's quite nice, look it up sometime) and has been coming to the big city his whole life.

With previous experience writing on topics ranging from the decision to drop the atomic bomb in World War Two, to the merits of Radiohead's eighth album, to how to properly dispose of household paints, Jeremy considers himself to be a pretty good writer. In his free time, Jeremy enjoys listening to Talking Heads, watching science-fiction movies, and eating really good pizza.
