Visiting from Chicago

By Ashley Villarreal
Published 2013-06-11 01:07:56
tweet

Grabbing a bite

Sharice and Gary were coming out of the American Museum of Natural History when a salesman for Central Park Guided Tours approached them. “Thank you, but not today,” said Sharice, smiling politely.

Views from the park

They were vacationing from Chicago and had enjoyed spending time at the museum where they saw Our Global Kitchen: Food, Nature, Culture. Sharice said home-cooked meals had been a big part of her life growing up and she'd been happy to see how food had impacted different societies throughout history.

Grabbing a bite

While they'd visited New York City many times before, they opted out of taking a stroll through Central Park because of the rain. However, they both agreed the areas around Belvedere Castle were among their favorites – mainly for the view.

Before visiting the museum they’d come from Brooklyn Diner, a local eatery near the southern edge of the park. Gary, who hails from Brooklyn, said that although the food was not like anything he'd grown up with, it had hit the spot for the moment.

The American Museum of Natural History is one of the largest and most celebrated museums in the worlds. Located on the Upper West Side, the museum contains 27 interconnecting buildings, with 45 permanet exhibition halls. With over 32 million specimens of plants, fossils, minerals, rocks, metorities, animals and much more. If you love science & culture, then the American Museum of Natural History is the place is be.
